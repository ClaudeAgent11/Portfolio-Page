CASE STUDY

Cortex — Enterprise AI & Customer Data Engine

Executive Manager, Data Strategy, Cortex & AI Services • Westpac • 2022–Present

The Challenge

Westpac's customer data was siloed across dozens of systems. Business teams couldn't access timely, unified customer insights. ML model deployments took 6-8 weeks. The bank needed a single enterprise customer view powered by AI — and a team to build and operate it.

The Approach

Designed and operationalised Cortex as an enterprise data product serving the full customer lifecycle — demographics, financials, ML propensity scores, and transaction data. Built a product operating model spanning 3 squads (~24 people) across engineering, data science, and analytics.

What I Shipped

  • MyEDB Heart: Customer insight flag enabling bankers to identify customers who use Westpac as their main financial institution — served via APIs to banker tools at scale
  • NHB Acquisition Model: ML model predicting next home buyers with strong accuracy (Mortgages hackathon)
  • Adaptive Change (MLOps): Reduced model deployment from weeks to days
  • AEP Integration: Connected Cortex model scores to Adobe Experience Platform for marketing campaigns
  • Project Hawkeye: Leading the replacement of customer behavioural scores from an external vendor to in-house ML models
  • Multiple ML models shipped end-to-end (Property Seller, MyEDB Loss, Salary Credit, EDB Upgrade, RRB)
  • Customer Obsession Framework: Led the design and rollout of the enterprise-wide Customer Obsession framework — aligning data products to customer outcomes and embedding customer-centric thinking across squads
  • Data Products Operating Model: Designed and operationalised the data products operating model now embedded across Westpac Group — defining how data products are built, governed, and consumed at enterprise scale
  • ERD Data Product: Launched the Enterprise Reference Data product, establishing a foundational shared data asset across the organisation

CASE STUDY

Banking-as-a-Service — Greenfield Digital Banking Platform

Product Owner → Head of Deposit Products • Westpac BaaS (Project Parsifal) • 2019–2022

Built the product capability and created multiple new-to-world deposit products from scratch on a cloud-native core banking platform, making Westpac the first Big Four bank in Australia to launch a BaaS offering.

The Challenge

Consumer banking was shifting. Fintechs and non-bank platforms were embedding financial services into their own ecosystems, and traditional banks risked being disintermediated. Westpac recognised the need to build a purpose-built digital banking platform — operating under the bank's licence — that would allow third-party partners to distribute Westpac-issued products via their own mobile apps and digital experiences. The initiative, internally known as Project Parsifal, was a greenfield venture: no legacy systems, no precedent in Australia.

The Approach

The platform was built on 10X Future Technologies' cloud-native core banking stack, providing real-time, API-first infrastructure. Partners owned the "glass" (front-end UX), while Westpac handled the ledger, regulatory compliance, and product manufacturing.

My role spanned two phases — build and commercialise:

Phase 1 — Build

Product Owner, Product Stream

Dec 2019 – Apr 2021

Reporting to the Chief Platform Officer. Responsible for building the transaction and savings product capability on the 10X platform — designing products to be configurable and partner-ready from day one.

Phase 2 — Commercialise

Head of Deposit Products

May 2021 – Jun 2022

Promoted to report to the Chief Product Officer. Accountable for creating, commercialising, launching and managing the transaction and savings products. Owned the product roadmap, P&L performance, and partner launch programmes.

What I Built — Platform Capability (0→1)

  • Core deposit product engine: Built the fundamental product infrastructure ground-up on 10X — bank account generation, bank statements, fund transfers, transaction views, balances, and credit interest calculations (actuarial audit-certified by a Big Four firm)
  • Product configuration tooling: Developed the capability to create, customise and launch products using a custom-built tool, enabling white-label partners to launch products within weeks with minimal IT involvement
  • Customer financial tools: Saving goals, spend budgets, auto sweeps, and transaction categorisation for spending insights — designed to help customers understand and manage their money
  • Transaction enrichment: Integrated with a specialist fintech for merchant transaction enrichment, transforming raw transaction data into meaningful customer insights

What I Launched — New Products for Partners (0→1)

Beyond building the platform capability, I created and launched multiple new-to-world banking products tailored for each partner's customers:

Afterpay Money Global BNPL fintech · 3.3M AU customers
  • Transaction account — everyday spending account with real-time push notifications
  • Savings account — goal-based savings with automated round-ups and sweeps
  • Cashflow management tools — budgeting, spend categorisation, and financial insights
SocietyOne Debt consolidation fintech · acquired by MoneyMe
  • Transaction account — everyday account complementing their personal loan offering
  • Savings account — deposit product deepening the customer relationship beyond lending

What I Learned

Building a greenfield banking platform is a rare and career-defining experience. The hardest lesson was that in a regulated 0→1 venture, the product isn't just the software — it's the entire value chain: compliance frameworks, partner contracts, interest calculation methodologies, and operational runbooks. You're not just building features; you're building an institution. The partners who succeed are the ones you've made it easy for — and "easy" is the result of enormous complexity hidden beneath the surface.

CASE STUDY

Directshares — Retail Share Trading Product & Platform

Senior Product Manager, P&L Owner • St.George / Westpac • 2017–2019

Owned the P&L for St.George's retail share trading product, managed the product lifecycle, and led a complex multi-stakeholder platform migration from ANZ to CMC Markets — safeguarding $10M in annual revenue.

The Challenge

St.George Directshares was a retail share trading product serving thousands of active traders, generating ~$10M in annual revenue. As Senior Product Manager and P&L owner, I was responsible for the commercial performance and product strategy of the platform — from pricing and feature roadmap to vendor relationships and customer retention. The most significant challenge during my tenure was migrating the entire customer base and trading infrastructure from the legacy ANZ platform to CMC Markets Stockbroking, while maintaining revenue and zero trading disruption.

The Approach

Managed the product end-to-end: P&L ownership, product roadmap, commercial negotiations, and the platform migration — coordinating across CMC Markets, St.George brand, Westpac technology, legal, compliance, and operations.

  • P&L ownership: Full commercial accountability — pricing strategy, revenue forecasting, cost management, and performance reporting to senior leadership
  • Stakeholder orchestration: Managed relationships across CMC Markets (external platform provider), St.George Bank (brand owner), and Westpac Group (parent company) — aligning commercial terms, technical integration, and customer experience
  • Customer migration strategy: Designed the migration approach to minimise disruption — phased rollout, proactive customer communications, and dedicated support channels
  • Platform evaluation: Assessed CMC Markets' trading capabilities against customer needs — ensuring feature parity for watchlists, portfolio management, domestic & international trading, warrants, ETFs, and conditional orders

What I Shipped

  • Full platform migration: Successfully moved the entire Directshares customer base from ANZ to CMC Markets Stockbroking infrastructure
  • Mobile app (iOS): St.George Directshares app — 4.7★ rating (137 ratings) — featuring live quotes, charts, market news, and full trading capability
  • Trading platform: Modern web-based trading interface under St.George branding, powered by CMC Markets — supporting domestic shares, international markets, warrants, and ETFs
  • Market research integration: Embedded research tools and market insights directly into the trading experience

The Product — St.George Directshares App

The Impact

$10M Annual revenue safeguarded

What I Learned

Platform migrations are deceptively complex — the technology is the easy part. The real challenge is managing the human side: customer trust, stakeholder alignment across organisational boundaries, and commercial negotiations that keep everyone's interests aligned. When $10M in revenue is at stake, the product manager's job is to be the calm centre of a very complex storm.

CASE STUDY

Cash Investment Account & WOI OneClick — From Product Launch to Digital Transformation

Senior Business Analyst → Product Manager & Product Owner • Westpac Online Investing • 2010–2016

Launched an award-winning cash product generating ~$2B in new FUM, then drove the digital transformation that doubled customer acquisition and unlocked ~$5M in annual revenue.

The Challenge

Westpac's online investing platform lacked a compelling cash product to deepen wallet share and attract new investors. Account opening was manual, paper-heavy, and slow — with high abandonment, poor STP rates, and a process that failed to leverage data the bank already held. The platform needed both a product innovation and a distribution transformation.

The Approach

My role spanned two phases — product launch and digital transformation:

Phase 1 — Launch

Senior Business Analyst, Westpac Institutional Bank

Jul 2010 – Apr 2011

Business stream lead responsible for capturing requirements, design, development and implementation of the new cash product. Integrated banking and investing to boost wallet share.

Phase 2 — Transform & Scale

Product Manager & Product Owner, Online Investing

Apr 2011 – Dec 2016

Promoted to lead product strategy, digital transformation, and the WOI OneClick program — championing the end-to-end digitisation of customer acquisition and formulating the pricing strategy that drove ~$5M in annual revenue.

What I Shipped

Phase 1 — Cash Investment Account (0→1)

  • Launched Westpac Cash Investment Account (CIA): Integrated banking and investing into a single product to boost wallet share and generate ~$2B in new Funds Under Management
  • Award-winning product design: Customer-centric approach resulting in Financial Review Smart Investor Blue Ribbon Award and Canstar Award
  • Established operations team: Built a new operations function from scratch, implementing change strategy, management, and communication plan for seamless process implementation

Phase 2 — Digital Transformation (WOI OneClick)

  • Business case & strategic roadmap: Developed the WOI OneClick digital transformation program, leveraging competitor analysis, industry research, and customer testing
  • Championed OneClick from strategy to execution: Doubled new accounts, increased bank penetration, and delivered significant revenue uplift
  • Digitised customer journey: Paperless signature-free account opening, bank integration, data pre-population, electronic verification & real-time account activation
  • Customer-centric responsive form: Built on a new forms platform with pre-population of customer data, online banking integration, and straight-through real-time account activation across web, mobile, and tablet — subsequently adopted by wider business units across the bank
  • Pricing architecture: Introduced CIA as the default settlement account at $19.95/trade vs $29.95 — a lever that has since been sharpened to $4.95/trade as the product matured, generating ~$5M in additional annual revenue
  • Cross-functional governance: Senior leadership, digital, product, marketing, technology, legal, compliance, change management, operations, and third-party vendors

What I Learned

The CIA story taught me that great products aren't just about the initial launch — they're about the ecosystem you build around them. CIA started as a standalone cash product, became a pricing lever for the OneClick platform, and ultimately drove a digital transformation that reshaped how the bank acquired customers. The product is still live on westpac.com.au a decade later — the best product decisions compound over time.
